Welcome to Sexual Harassment Awareness!



Women's sexual harassment needs attention because it is a pervasive and deeply concerning issue that affects millions of women worldwide. Sexual harassment refers to any unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, or other verbal, non-verbal, or physical conduct of a sexual nature that creates a hostile, intimidating, or offensive environment. Addressing women's sexual harassment is essential to protect their rights, well-being, and dignity. It requires collective action, education, and the establishment of robust policies and support systems to create a society where women can live, work, and thrive without the fear of harassment.

What can I do when I witness sexual harassment?

You may have heard the term bystander intervention to describe stepping in to help
if you see someone who might be in danger or at risk for sexual assault.
Bystander intervention can also be a helpful strategy
if you witness sexual harassment. You don’t have to be a hero to make
a positive impact in someone’s life, and you can intervene
in a way that fits your comfort level and is appropriate for the situation.
If you choose to step in, you may be
able to give the person being harassed a chance to get
to a safe place or leave the situation. Below are some of the steps
you can take if you see someone being sexually harassed—just remember to C.A.R.E., and of course,
keep your own safety in mind at all times.

Create a distraction Do what you can to interrupt the harassment, or distract those taking part in the harassment.
But remember to make sure that you aren’t putting yourself in danger by doing this.
If someone seems like they could become violent, do not draw their attention.
Ask directly Talk directly with the person who is being harassed.
If they are being harassed at work or school, offer to accompany them anytime they have to meet with the harasser.
If a friend is worried about walking alone to their car at night, offer to walk with them.
Refer to an authority The safest way to intervene for both you and the person being harassed may be to bring in an authority figure.
You can talk to another employee, security guard, RA in your dorm, bartender, or bouncer, and they will often be willing to step in.
Enlist others It can be hard to step in alone, especially if you are worried about your own safety or if you don’t think you will be able to help on your own.
It may be a good idea to enlist the help of a friend or another bystander.

Causes

Sexual harassment is a complex issue with multiple underlying causes. It is important to note that the responsibility for sexual harassment lies solely with the perpetrators, and no individual's actions or characteristics justify such behavior. However, there are several factors that can contribute to the prevalence of sexual harassment against women. Here are some common factors:

  1. Power imbalances: Sexual harassment often occurs in situations where there is a power disparity, such as the workplace, educational institutions, or other hierarchical environments. Perpetrators may exploit their positions of power or authority to exert control over others.
  2. Gender inequality: Deep-rooted gender inequality perpetuates the notion that women are subordinate to men, which can contribute to an environment where sexual harassment is more likely to occur. Societal norms, stereotypes, and attitudes that devalue women's autonomy and agency can fuel such behavior.
  3. Cultural and social factors: Cultural norms and societal attitudes play a significant role in shaping behaviors and perceptions. Cultures that normalize or tolerate sexual objectification, misogyny, or victim-blaming can contribute to an environment conducive to sexual harassment.
  4. Lack of awareness and education: Insufficient education and awareness about consent, boundaries, and respectful behavior can contribute to the perpetuation of sexual harassment. When individuals lack understanding about what constitutes harassment or fail to recognize the importance of consent, they may engage in inappropriate behavior.
  5. Organizational factors: In some cases, organizational cultures may inadvertently contribute to a climate where sexual harassment thrives. Lack of proper policies, inadequate reporting mechanisms, failure to address complaints effectively, and a culture of silence or victim-blaming can deter individuals from reporting incidents or seeking justice.
  6. Individual factors: While it is essential to understand that the responsibility for harassment lies with the perpetrator, individual factors such as entitlement, misogyny, lack of empathy, and distorted beliefs about power dynamics can contribute to someone engaging in harassing behavior.

It is crucial to address these underlying causes through a comprehensive approach that includes education, awareness campaigns, legal protections, supportive workplace policies, and cultural shifts towards gender equality and respect for all individuals. Creating a safe and inclusive society requires efforts from individuals, institutions, and society as a whole to eradicate sexual harassment and promote a culture of dignity, consent, and equality.

    Effects

    Women who experience sexual harassment can suffer a range of negative effects, both immediate and long-term. Here are some common effects of women's sexual harassment:

    1. Psychological impact: Sexual harassment can have significant psychological consequences on survivors. They may experience feelings of fear, anxiety, depression, and helplessness. It can lead to a loss of self-esteem, self-confidence, and a sense of safety. Survivors may also develop post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD) symptoms, including flashbacks, nightmares, and hypervigilance.
    2. Emotional distress: Sexual harassment can cause intense emotional distress for women. They may feel humiliated, ashamed, or guilty, even though they are not at fault. Survivors often experience anger, frustration, and a sense of powerlessness. These emotions can impact their overall well-being and quality of life.
    3. Physical health effects: The stress and trauma associated with sexual harassment can manifest in physical symptoms. Survivors may experience headaches, digestive issues, sleep disturbances, and other stress-related ailments. The long-term impact of chronic stress can also contribute to various health problems, including cardiovascular issues and compromised immune function.
    4. Occupational consequences: Sexual harassment can have adverse effects on a woman's career and professional life. It can create a hostile work environment, leading to decreased job satisfaction, reduced productivity, and impaired job performance. Survivors may feel compelled to change jobs or leave the workforce altogether due to the psychological toll or lack of support.
    5. Interpersonal relationships: Women who experience sexual harassment may struggle with trust and intimacy in their personal relationships. They may have difficulty forming or maintaining healthy relationships due to the emotional scars and the fear of vulnerability. Sexual harassment can also strain relationships with colleagues, friends, and family members.
    6. Self-blame and stigma: Survivors of sexual harassment may internalize blame and feel a sense of shame or guilt, even though they are not responsible for the harassment. This self-blame can perpetuate feelings of isolation and prevent survivors from seeking support or reporting

Delivery rider held for sexual harassment

By Emmanuel Tupas
- January 4, 2023 - 12:00am

MANILA, Philippines — A delivery rider accused of sexually harassing a woman for two months was arrested in Quezon City on Monday. Anthony Fernandez, 45, a rider of online delivery services company Lalamove, was collared in an entrapment operation along Congressional Avenue in Barangay Pasong Tamo. Fernandez’s arrest stemmed from the complaint of a 33-year-old woman that he has been harassing her since Nov. 7 last year. The victim, a customer service agent, said she has been receiving lewd messages from a cell phone number. She sought police assistance after Fernandez unwittingly sent another message on Monday using his official Lalamove account number. Charges of violating Republic Act 11313 or the Safe Spaces Act are being readied against Fernandez, police said.



5 Cavite teachers accused of sexual harassment face formal charges: DepEd

Jaehwa Bernardo
Posted at Sep 08 2022

MANILA (UPDATE) — The Department of Education has filed administrative charges against 5 of the 7 high school teachers accused of sexual harassment in Bacoor, Cavite, its spokesperson said Thursday. DepEd Spokesperson Michael Poa said the teachers were charged with grave misconduct, conduct prejudicial to the best interest of the service, and violation of the agency's child protection policy. The department is unable to issue charges against the 2 remaining teachers due to lack of evidence, Poa said. He reiterated his appeal for students to come forward if they experienced sexual harassment or abuse in their schools. "Naghihikayat tayo sa victims ng alleged sexual harassment na lumabas para makapagbigay ng kanilang affidavit, statement or ebidensiya at para mabigyan natin ng formal charge, hindi lang itong 7, pati na rin 'yong ibang sinasabing nag-commit ng sexual harassment," he said in a press briefing.



DepEd launches email, hotlines for school sexual abuse complaints

Jaehwa Bernardo
Posted at Sep 08 2022

MANILA (UPDATE) — The Department of Education launched Thursday new channels where students can report incidents of sexual harassment and abuse in schools. This is the latest effort of the education department to strengthen child protection in schools after at least 7 high school teachers in Bacoor, Cavite were accused of sexually harassing their students. Harassment and abuse victims can report directly to Education Secretary Sara Duterte's office via the email depedabusereport@gmail.com, DepEd said in a statement. Complainants can also reach Duterte's office through the phone numbers 8633-1942, 8635-9817 and 09959218461. "Direct line po siya kasi naintindihan natin 'yong embarrassment at sensitivity ng matter kapag pumupunta sila sa officials ng school," DepEd Spokesman Michael Poa said in a press conference. (We established a direct line because we understand the embarrassment that the victims feel and the sensitivity of the matter when they come forward to school officials.) In a statement, Enough is Enough, a group of students who experienced sexual harassment in schools, said it welcomed the DepEd's move to establish a hotline for campus abuse and harassment victims. "We hope that this is a permanent fixture and not a knee-jerk reaction that will not be sustained once the controversies die down. We [also] hope that only qualified individuals in psycho-social support should be part of its team and not enablers of predators that will only gaslight victims and exacerbate their trauma," the group said. The DepEd has filed administrative charges against 5 of the 7 Cavite teachers accused of sexual harassment.





SC suspends lawyer for two years for sexual harassment at work

By GMA Integrated News
Published May 3, 2023

The Supreme Court (SC) suspended a lawyer for two years for committing sexual advances on a colleague at the workplace. The high court imposed a two-year suspension from the practice of law on a senior partner in a law firm after a junior associate lawyer filed an administrative case. The SC said the respondent was charged with committing “sexually-laced acts ranging from dirty jokes, innuendos, inappropriate personal intimate questions about her romantic relationships, and sharing about his extramarital sexual acts/conquests, to actual sexual advances.” It added that the respondent also went confessing that he would have fallen for the complainant had they been within the same age range. “Sexual harassment in the workplace is not about a man taking advantage of a woman by reason of sexual desire—it is about power being exercised by a superior officer over his women subordinates,” the SC said.
